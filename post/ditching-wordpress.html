Ditching Wordpress for a newer blogging platform: Scriptogr.am, Jekyll, Pelican and Ghost

This site is powered by Scriptogr.am, a new blogging platform (currently in beta) that features Dropbox integration:

  • Posts are written in Markdown, and saved in the Apps/scriptogram/posts/ folder of a linked Dropbox account where they will automatically be “markdowned” (styled) to HTML and published to http://scriptogr.am/blogname. Draft posts saved in Apps/scriptogram/posts/drafts/ are not published.

  • The only overhead required for posts, at the minimum, are two plaintext lines at the top of each:

---
layout: post
title: Title goes here
date: YYYY-mm-dd timestamp

Oh what a relief it is to be able to write a post without having to log in to Wordpress or use its syntax. And if you didn’t already it notice, it just got much easier blog on a mobile device by pushing the post to Scriptogr.am.

Personally, I love the recent direction blogging platforms have taken. For those that care more about blogging,

“Wordpress has grown up. It’s not really about blogging anymore. It’s moved on to be about websites and content management… Wordpress, by all rights, is no longer just a blogging platform.” — John O’Nolan, founder of Ghost.

And as much I admire the power of Wordpress, I love kicking it old school with a static site. Having a static blog simplifies the hosting of it and reduces security risks. Wordpress is symptomatic of the times in which it was created, when LAMP stacks and database backends were used in every web development project even when they were perhaps unnecessary.

Along with many bloggers who have dropped Wordpress in favor of a static blog generators have found, the ability to drop the use of a database breathes fresh air into blogging. I chose Scriptogr.am after trialling with Octopress/Jekyll (written in Ruby)1 and Pelican (written in Python)2.

I’ve yet to try Ghost, the exciting new blogging platform “made for love”. The open source Kickstarter project is currently at version 0.3.1 at time of writing (Oct 2013), but is yet to be released to anyone that wasn’t part of their initial funding.

This site

One feature I’m missing from my previous WordPress site is the ability to have it bilingual, as was made possible by the third-party Wordpress plugin qTranslate.

I have no regrets about losing my bilingual site (if I were in or writing this in Québec I most sincerely would be); my impression of Scriptogr.am, static blog generators in general, and my freedom from at least one database, greatly outweighs my allegiance to Wordpress. I have hope Scriptogr.am and/or Ghost will provide multilingual capabilities in the future, but in the mean time and until then, sorry francophones,
